# Healthcare Digital Implementation Case Study

A small self-directed project by **Rameez Amjad**.


This case looks at implementing a digital referral and appointment management tool into an NHS outpatient service.

It is not based on internal company information and does not use real patient data.

## Scenario

An outpatient service is introducing a digital tool to help manage referrals, triage, appointment booking, patient communication, clinic outcomes and reporting.

The challenge is not just switching on software. The challenge is making sure admin staff, clinicians and managers actually use it, trust it, and feel that it reduces work rather than adding another system.

## Workflow

Referral received
→ admin validation
→ clinical triage
→ appointment booked
→ patient notified
→ clinic takes place
→ outcome documented
→ follow-up, discharge or further tests arranged
→ manager reviews waiting list and capacity

## Pain points

* Referrals may arrive with missing or unclear information.
* Admin teams may rely on manual chasing and spreadsheets.
* Clinical triage may be delayed.
* Patients may be booked into the wrong clinic type.
* DNAs and cancellations may not be acted on quickly.
* Managers may not have a clear view of capacity and backlog.
* Staff may keep using old workarounds if the new tool feels slower.

## Implementation approach

The first step would be discovery: speaking to admin staff, clinicians, service managers and operational leads to understand how the pathway currently works.

Then I would map the workflow, identify manual workarounds, agree baseline metrics, select a small pilot area, train users around their real tasks, track adoption after go-live, and feed structured feedback back to the product or configuration team.

## Metrics I would track

* referral-to-triage time
* time from referral to appointment booking
* DNA / cancellation rate
* booking error rate
* clinic utilisation
* documentation completion time
* active usage by staff group
* number of manual workarounds still being used

## What I am trying to show

This project is not meant to pretend I have already led this type of implementation. It is meant to show the questions I would ask: how work currently happens, where delays occur, who the users are, what adoption depends on, and how to measure whether the rollout is actually helping.
